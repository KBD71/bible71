Doctrine Exposition (교리 해설)

In our previous studies, we explored how justification, adoption, sanctification, and saving faith are the sovereign and free gifts of God's grace. In today's confession, we confront an essential corollary of these redemptive blessings: repentance unto life is fundamentally an evangelical grace. It is not a heavy, legalistic work designed to purchase God's love, but a beautiful and refreshing fruit of the Gospel of Jesus Christ. Because it is a sovereign gift, it must be robustly and continuously proclaimed from the Christian pulpit.

To preach the Gospel faithfully means that ministers must never sever the doctrine of faith from the doctrine of repentance. A pulpit that only emphasizes justification by faith while ignoring repentance inevitably births the toxic spiritual condition known as cheap grace. This distortions leads individuals into antinomianism (lawlessness)—the erroneous belief that because they are saved by grace, they are free to continue living in unrepentant, self-indulgent sin without ever feeling a holy hatred for their offenses. True saving faith and genuine gospel repentance are twin graces; they are distinct yet entirely inseparable.

The Dangerous Hazards of Omitting Repentance:

  • Nurturing False Assurance: When repentance is left unproclaimed, church members may rest on a mere intellectual assent to historical facts without possessing a true, heart-transforming trust in Christ.
  • Distorting the Nature of Faith: Saving faith is not static; it is active. True faith involves dynamic, wholehearted trust which automatically manifests as a painful turning away from sin and turning toward God.
  • Weakening the Believer's Walk: Repentance is not a one-time event at conversion but a continuous lifestyle for the Christian. Daily sanctification is impossible without the recurrent practice of turning back to God's mercy.

Thus, when ministers preach repentance toward God and faith in Jesus Christ, they are preserving the biblical equilibrium of the Gospel. Every believer is invited to actively take part in this self-examination. As the apostle Paul admonishes, we must test ourselves to see whether we are truly in the faith (2 Cor 13:5). Let us run continuously to the cross, hating our sin and walking in the joy of new obedience.

Vocabulary 01

Evangelical grace

율법의 공포가 아닌 '복음적 은혜'

여기서 Evangelical은 '복음적인'이라는 뜻으로, 행위나 율법의 엄격한 요구 아래에서 일어나는 강제적 참회가 아니라, 예수 그리스도의 대속과 하나님의 용서하시는 사랑을 깨닫고 마음 깊은 곳에서 저절로 우러나오는 참회와 은혜를 뜻합니다.
Vocabulary 02

Whereof

고전 영문법의 관계부사 (그것의, ~의)

현대 영어의 of which와 동일한 고어체적 부사입니다. "the doctrine whereof"는 앞의 'repentance unto life'를 수식하여 "그 회개에 대한 교리"라는 뜻으로 매끄럽게 해석됩니다.
Vocabulary 03

As well as

~뿐만 아니라, ~와 마찬가지로

문장 끝부분의 "as well as that of faith in Christ"는 그리스도를 믿는 믿음의 교리를 설교하는 것과 동등하게 회개의 교리도 철저하고 필수적으로 설교해야 한다는 균형적 강령을 표명합니다.
Theology Focus

Intellectual Assent

단순한 지적 동의 (Notitia & Assensus)

단순히 역사적 복음의 정보를 지적으로 맞다고 동의(Assent)하는 단계입니다. 참된 구원 신앙은 이 단계를 넘어, 전인격적 '신뢰(Fiducia)'가 동반되어 반드시 삶의 회개와 돌이킴을 열매로 나타냅니다.
Grammar Focus

Is to be preached

'be + to 부정사' (의무/수동 수식)

여기서 be + to 부정사 용법은 **의무(obligation)**를 강하게 나타냅니다. "반드시 설교되어야 한다"는 뜻으로, 복음 사역자들의 절대적인 강단 임무를 지칭하는 명확하고 엄숙한 표현입니다.
