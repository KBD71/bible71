Westminster Standards (신앙고백서 및 요리문답)

English Text

Q. 87. What is repentance unto life?

"Repentance unto life is a saving grace, whereby a sinner, out of a true sense of his sin, and apprehension of the mercy of God in Christ, doth, with grief and hatred of his sin, turn from it unto God, with full purpose of, and endeavor after, new obedience."

문 87. 생명에 이르는 회개는 무엇입니까?

English Text

Q. 76. What is repentance unto life?

"Repentance unto life is a saving grace, wrought in the heart of a sinner by the Spirit and Word of God, whereby, out of the sight and sense, not only of the danger, but also of the filthiness and odiousness of his sins, and upon the apprehension of God’s mercy in Christ to such as are penitent, he so grieves for and hates his sins, as that he turns from them all unto God, purposing and endeavoring constantly to walk with him in all the ways of new obedience."

Korean translation (한글 번역)

문 76. 생명에 이르는 회개는 무엇입니까?

"생명에 이르는 회개는 성령 하나님과 말씀이 죄인의 마음속에 일으키신 구원의 은혜입니다. 이로써 죄인은 자신의 죄가 위험할 뿐만 아니라, 더럽고 혐오스럽다는 것을 보고 자각하며, 또한 참회하는 자에게 그리스도 안에서 베푸시는 하나님의 긍휼을 깨닫습니다. 따라서 자신의 죄를 몹시 슬퍼하며 미워하고, 모든 죄에서 하나님께로 돌이켜 새로운 순종의 모든 길에 끊임없이 하나님과 동행하기로 의도하고 애씁니다."

Doctrine Exposition (교리 해설)

We have previously observed how justification, adoption, sanctification, and saving faith are free gifts of divine favor. Today, we delve into another critical element of salvation: repentance unto life. Although repentance manifests as a human response—meaning we are the ones who actively regret and turn away from sin—it is essentially a supernatural saving grace. Left to our own devices, humanity is incapable of producing the holy, transforming sorrow that leads to life.

To understand this, we must contrast true biblical repentance with secular guilt or raw remorse. The Scripture provides a vivid example in the tragic end of Judas Iscariot. When Judas saw that Jesus was condemned, he was gripped with deep regret. He returned the silver, confessed his guilt openly ("I have sinned in betraying innocent blood"), and even manifested intense emotional weight. Yet, his experience did not lead to life. His remorse was merely self-condemnation; it lacked an apprehension of the mercy of God in Christ. Ultimately, he despaired and destroyed himself.

Compare this with the apostle Simon Peter. After denying Christ three times, Peter wept bitterly. However, his grief was not fatalistic. Peter’s sorrow was saturated with faith in Christ’s ultimate mercy, leading him back to the savior for restoration and eventually culminating in a beautifully renewed pastoral commission. This demonstrates that true gospel repentance always moves toward the Cross, never away from it.

Three Indispensable Dynamics of True Repentance:

  1. A True Sense of Sin’s Filthiness: It is not merely a fear of punishment, but a deep spiritual realization that sin is filthy and odious—entirely hostile to the holy nature and righteous law of God. Like Isaiah, the penitent cries out, "Woe is me, for I am ruined!" (Isa 6:5).
  2. Apprehension of Divine Mercy: True repentance is fueled by the beauty of God's grace in Christ. Without realizing God's willingness to forgive, our hearts harden under guilt. Like the Prodigal Son returning home, we may feel unworthy to be called sons, but we are magnetically drawn by the father's ready, status-restoring love.
  3. An Active Pursuit of New Obedience: True conversion changes direction. A penitent actively turns from all sin to pursue a life of new obedience. We walk with Him in all the ways of His commandments, driven not by legalistic efforts to buy God’s favor, but by profound gratitude for the grace we have already received (Rom 12:1).

Therefore, the new obedience that accompanies repentance is not the *cause* of our acceptance before God, but the *fruit* of it. Because God's mercy is so vast, we are inspired to present our bodies as living sacrifices, conforming our lives to His Spirit and walking in His paths with gratitude.

Vocabulary 01

Repentance unto life

생명에 이르는 참된 회개

신학적으로 이 회개는 일시적 후회나 세상적 근심(사망에 이르는 후회)과 대비됩니다. 성령의 주권적 사역으로 말미암아 영생에 이르도록 마음 전체가 하나님께로 돌이켜지는 '구원적 은혜'를 지칭합니다.
Vocabulary 02

Apprehension

이해, 자각, 깨달음 및 영접

여기서 'apprehension'은 단순한 지적 동의를 넘어서, 하나님의 자비하심을 영혼 깊이 깨닫고 인격적으로 받아들이며 신뢰함을 뜻하는 고전적이고 풍성한 단어입니다.
Vocabulary 03

Filthiness & Odiousness

죄의 더러움과 혐오성

Filthiness는 도덕적 불결함을 뜻하며, Odiousness는 하나님 앞에 가증하고 역겨운 상태를 뜻합니다. 참회자는 죄를 단순한 실수나 규칙 위반이 아닌, 거룩하신 하나님 앞에 가증한 오물처럼 느끼게 됩니다.
Vocabulary 04

Penitent

뉘우치는 사람, 회개하는 자

형용사로는 '뉘우치고 참회하는' 뜻이지만 명사로 쓰여 '참회하고 회개에 동참한 죄인'을 뜻합니다. 그리스도 안에 담긴 하나님의 긍휼은 바로 이 참회하는 자(penitent)들을 위한 것입니다.
Grammar Focus

Whereby, out of the...

관계부사와 전치사구의 복잡한 수식

whereby는 '그것에 의해서(=by which)'라는 뜻으로 구원의 은혜를 보충 수식합니다. 이 관계절 안에서 out of (~로부터 유래하여)가 동사 'turn'을 수식하며 복잡한 문장 구조를 이룹니다. 천천히 구문을 쪼개서 읽는 연습이 필요합니다.
Grammar Focus 2

...so grieves... as that he turns

'so ~ as to / so ~ as that' 구조

'그의 죄를 매우 깊이 슬퍼하고 미워하여 마침내 그 결과로서 하나님께로 돌이킨다'는 강한 인과적 관계 및 결과를 표현하는 고전적 문체 구조입니다.
