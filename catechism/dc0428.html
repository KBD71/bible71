Participation in Christ's Redemption

Shorter Catechism 29; Larger Catechism 58

Doctrine Audio Guide

W. Shorter Catechism 29

Q: How are we made partakers of the redemption purchased by Christ?

A: We are made partakers of the redemption purchased by Christ, by the effectual application of it to us by his Holy Spirit.

W. Larger Catechism 58

Q: How do we come to be made partakers of the benefits which Christ hath procured?

A: We are made partakers of the benefits which Christ hath procured, by the application of them to us, which is the work especially of the Holy Spirit.

Key Scriptures

"...he saved us through the washing of rebirth and renewal by the Holy Spirit, whom he poured out on us generously through Jesus Christ our Savior." (Titus 3:5-6)

"Yet to all who did receive him, to those who believed in his name, he gave the right to become children of God." (John 1:12)

01. Participation in the Benefits of Redemption

Redemption is the work where Christ paid the debt of our sins on the cross to free us from slavery to Satan. However, this redemption is meaningless to us unless it is personally applied.

  • ! We do not participate in the *act* of redemption (which is Christ's work alone), but in its fruits: justification, adoption, sanctification, and the assurance of God's love.
  • ! As stated in WLC 58, "participation in redemption" is synonymous with "participation in its benefits." It encompasses everything from the peace of conscience to the final perseverance of the saints.

02. The Sovereign Work of the Holy Spirit

Applying the benefits of Christ is not something we can achieve through our own will, wisdom, or strength. Because we are spiritually dead, we cannot even understand the things of God on our own.

The ascended Christ sends the Holy Spirit—the "other Comforter"—to effectively bridge the gap between the work finished on the cross and the individual believer's heart. This is a supernatural application, ensuring that what Christ purchased is actually delivered to those He intended to save.

03. The Meaning of Effectual Application

The term "effectual" is crucial. It distinguishes the Spirit's work from a mere invitation that could be accepted or rejected by human whim.

  • Not a Mere Offer: If the Spirit's work only provided a "possibility" of salvation, it would not be described as "effectual."
  • Irresistible Grace: God's Word does not return void (Isaiah 55:11). When the Spirit applies redemption, He changes the heart so that the person willingly and certainly believes.
  • Certainty of Salvation: From predestination to glorification (Romans 8:30), the chain is unbreakable because the Holy Spirit's application is sovereign and guaranteed to succeed.
