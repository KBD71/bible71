Doctrine Exposition (교리 해설)

Today’s standard from the Westminster Confession of Faith (WCF) 15.5 addresses a critical, practical dimension of our spiritual lives: the absolute concreteness of repentance. The confession explicitly states that "Men ought not to content themselves with a general repentance." Simply reciting a generic formula such as "I am a sinner" is not sufficient, for true, saving faith demands that each person meticulously examine their heart and strive to repent of their particular sins, particularly. This targets the perennial human temptation to gloss over personal guilt using vague generalizations, hiding specific acts of rebellion behind abstract moral categories.

Historically, this theological formulation served as a direct refutation of two distinct errors. First, it countered the Roman Catholic system of ritualistic penance, which during the late medieval period often reduced repentance to a mechanical duty. In this system, participation in the sacraments, such as attending Mass or receiving priestly absolution, was believed to apply grace through the mere performance of the rite—a concept known as ex opere operato (사효성). The Reformers strongly rejected this physical-only mechanism, asserting that mechanical ritual cannot substitute for a heart that is internally transformed and actively mourning over specific transgressions.

Second, this standard opposes the dangerous error of antinomianism (율법폐기론). Antinomians mistakenly argue that because believers are fully justified by grace through faith in Christ, they are entirely liberated from the moral law as a rule of life. Consequently, they devalue personal repentance, treating it as either unnecessary or merely superficial. Against this, the confession declares that a genuine confession must be thorough, transparent, and direct. The transformation of Zacchaeus beautifully illustrates this level of concreteness (Luke 19:8). He did not stop at declaring generic sorrow; he immediately specified his financial crimes and pledged a fourfold restitution, proving that true repentance acts with surgical precision.

The Practical Question of Hidden Sins

If we are required to repent of our sins "particularly," what happens to the sins we have forgotten, or those we are entirely unaware of?

  • Limitation of Human Knowledge: Humans are not omniscient. Sins of ignorance and forgotten faults inevitably exist, and the Confession does not demand an impossible, exhaustive listing of every subconscious flaw to secure salvation.
  • Sincerity Over Perfection: What God requires is a posture of absolute honesty. We must not deliberately conceal, minimize, or ignore any sin that the Holy Spirit brings to our conscious awareness.
  • Active Self-Examination: True repentance is fueled by active, scriptural self-scrutiny (Psalm 139:23-24). It invites the searching light of God to expose hidden motives, transforming general, lazy admissions into detailed, honest confessions.

Ultimately, WCF 15.5 frames this painstaking work as "every man's duty." This duty, however, is not a grim, legalistic exercise designed to induce morbid introspection or spiritual despair. Far from being a source of misery, concrete repentance is the gateway to profound joy and lasting assurance. When we hide our specific offenses, our inner lives suffer under the heavy conviction of God's hand (Psalm 32:3-4). But when we step into the light, naming our sins one by one and laying them at the foot of the cross, we experience the deep, liberating reality of God's forgiveness (Psalm 32:5). Sincere, detailed confession reconciles our daily communion with the Father and restores the sweet "joy of salvation" (Psalm 51:12).

Theological & English Notes (신학 및 영어 학습 노트)

Grammar Focus 01

Ought not to content themselves with...

"~에 만족해서는 안 된다" (자기만족 금지의 표현)

ought not to + 동사원형은 도덕적 의무를 나타내는 표현입니다. content oneself with...는 수동적으로 어떠한 선에서 "만족하다, 자위하다"라는 뜻으로, 피상적인 회개 수준에만 머물러 있으려는 인간의 태도를 경고하는 장엄한 문어체적 어휘입니다.
Theology Focus 02

Ex Opere Operato

사효성 (일 자체의 행함으로 인해 발생하는 효력)

라틴어로 "행해진 일 자체로부터"라는 뜻으로, 천주교 성례론의 핵심입니다. 세례나 미사 등의 의식이 집례될 때, 참여하는 개인의 신앙이나 마음 상태와 상관없이 기계적이고 물리적으로 은혜가 주입된다는 이론입니다. 종교개혁자들은 성령을 통한 참된 믿음과 회개 없는 이러한 '기계적 구원관'을 철저히 거부했습니다.
Grammar Focus 03

Endeavor to repent

"회개하기를 온 힘을 다해 애쓰다" (노력과 의지)

endeavor는 단순히 한 번 시도하는 것을 넘어, 구체적인 목표를 향해 정성과 온 힘을 기울여 노력하고 애쓰는 행동(strive, struggle)을 의미합니다. 회개가 감정의 우발적 고조가 아니라, 인격적이고 부단한 영적 투쟁의 과정임을 강조하는 단어입니다.
Theology Focus 04

Antinomianism

율법폐기론 / 도덕폐기론

그리스어 anti(~에 반대하여)와 nomos(율법)에서 유래한 용어로, 믿음으로 구원받았으니 신자는 더 이상 구체적인 도덕법을 따르거나 회개할 의무가 없다고 주장하는 신학적 오류입니다. 본문은 이 율법폐기론이 가져오는 피상적 신앙을 강력하게 비판합니다.
Grammar Focus 05

Particular sins, particularly

"특정한 죄들을 낱낱이/구체적으로" (중복적 강조)

동일한 어원인 형용사 particular(특별한, 구체적인)와 부사 particularly(낱낱이, 개별적으로)를 병렬 배치하여, 회개의 대상과 회개 방식 모두가 지독할 정도로 선명하고 확실해야 함을 수사학적으로 보여주는 유서 깊은 고어 문체입니다.
