Westminster Confession of Faith 9.1

[English Version]

God hath endued the will of man with that natural liberty, that it is neither forced, nor by any absolute necessity of nature determined to good or evil.

[Korean Version]

하나님께서 사람의 의지에 본성의 자유를 부여하셨다. 이 본성의 자유는 강요받지 않으며 또한 본성의 어떤 절대적인 필연성 때문에 선이나 악을 행하도록 결정되어 있지도 않다.

01. The Faculty of Natural Liberty

Free will, as defined in WCF 9.1, refers to the capacity of a person—as a whole being with reason, emotion, and volition—to make choices and decisions. This natural liberty is an inherent gift granted by God to the human soul.

Crucially, this faculty remains even after the Fall. While the ability to choose spiritual good is corrupted, the essential nature of the will to act according to its own desire is not lost. Every human being remains a free moral agent, not a mechanical object.

02. Free Will is Neither Forced nor Determined

The primary characteristic of this liberty is that it is "not forced." This applies to both external and internal dimensions. Externally, while circumstances may influence a person, the will itself remains the ultimate seat of choice. Internally, the will is not "forced" by the intellect; it acts in harmony with what the mind perceives as desirable.

The Confession also rejects Determinism—the idea that humans are programmed by an absolute mechanical necessity to choose either good or evil. Whether choosing a virtuous path or a sinful one, the person acts spontaneously and of their own accord.

03. Harmony between Nature and Responsibility

To understand this logic, consider the nature of God. God always does what is good because He is inherently good. However, His goodness does not "force" Him; He chooses the good most freely according to His nature.

  • Moral Agency: Humans act according to their nature (sinful or regenerate) yet do so without external compulsion.
  • Inescapable Responsibility: Because choices are voluntary, humans are justly held accountable for their actions. We cannot plead "destiny" as an excuse for sin.

Theological & English Notes

1. Endue with / Endow with (부여하다)

'God hath endued...'에서 'hath'는 'has'의 고어이며, 'endue'는 '부여하다'라는 뜻입니다. 인간의 자유의지는 스스로 만든 것이 아니라 창조주로부터 받은 선물임을 강조합니다.

2. Neither ... nor (A도 아니고 B도 아니다)

강요받는 것도 아니고(neither forced), 결정되어 있는 것도 아니다(nor determined)라는 구조로, 자유의지의 자발성을 정의할 때 쓰이는 핵심적인 부정적 정의 방식입니다.

3. Spontaneously (자발적으로 / 자연스럽게)

외부의 기계적 원인에 의해서가 아니라, 주체의 내적 동기에 의해 선택이 일어남을 뜻합니다. 개혁주의 신학은 이 '자발성'을 자유의지의 본질로 봅니다.

4. Determinism (결정론)

모든 사건이 이전에 결정된 원인에 의해 필연적으로 일어난다는 주장입니다. 신앙고백서는 인간이 기계적으로 움직이는 존재라는 이러한 결정론을 배격합니다.
