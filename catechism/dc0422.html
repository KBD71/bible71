Westminster Confession of Faith 9.2

[English Version]

Man, in his state of innocency, had freedom, and power to will and to do that which was good and well pleasing to God; but yet, mutably, so that he might fall from it.

[Korean Version]

무죄 상태에서 사람은 하나님 보시기에 선하고 기쁨이 되는 것을 원하고 행할 자유와 능력을 가지고 있었다. 그럼에도 이것은 가변적이어서, 사람이 타락할 수도 있었다.

01. The Capacity for Spiritual Good

In the state of innocency, the human will was not neutral but was positively inclined toward the good. God created man in His own image, endowing him with knowledge, righteousness, and true holiness. Therefore, Adam possessed the actual power to obey God's commands and live in a manner pleasing to Him.

This is often described as Posse non peccare (the ability not to sin). Unlike our fallen state today, Adam was not enslaved to sinful desires and could have remained in perfect fellowship with God through his own free choices.

02. The Mystery of Mutability

While man had the power to do good, this state was mutable (changeable). Adam was perfect in his creation but not yet confirmed in immutable holiness. God allowed man to act according to the liberty of his own will, which included the possibility of choosing against the divine command.

The shift from obedience to disobedience was a change in the exercise of the will. When Adam chose to listen to the serpent’s deception rather than God’s truth, his will freely moved from its original orientation toward God to an orientation toward self.

03. Liberty vs. Indifference

It is a mistake to think of Adam as being in a state of "indifference" where he was equally likely to choose good or evil. He was created upright (Ecclesiastes 7:29).

  • Inherent Goodness: His initial disposition was to love God, as the law was written on his heart.
  • Spontaneous Choice: His fall was not a mechanical failure but a spontaneous and free act of turning away from the source of his life.
  • Accountability: Because he possessed the power to remain upright, his fall was without excuse, demonstrating the weight of human responsibility.

Theological & English Notes

1. State of Innocency (무죄 상태)

아담이 타락하기 이전, 죄의 오염이 전혀 없는 상태를 의미합니다. 'Innocence'는 단순히 잘못이 없다는 뜻을 넘어, 하나님과의 완벽한 조화 속에 있는 상태를 뜻합니다.

2. Mutably (가변적으로 / 변하기 쉽게)

'Mutable'은 '변할 수 있는'이라는 뜻입니다. 창조된 인간의 의지는 선을 행할 능력이 있었지만, 하나님의 은혜로 고정된 상태는 아니었기에 스스로 변하여 타락할 가능성을 포함하고 있었습니다.

3. "Well pleasing to God" (하나님을 기쁘시게 하는)

성경적 순종의 핵심을 보여주는 표현입니다. 단순히 율법을 기계적으로 지키는 것이 아니라, 하나님의 성품과 뜻에 부합하여 그분이 기뻐하시는 결과를 낳는 것을 의미합니다.

4. Endow with... (~을 부여하다)

하나님께서 인간을 창조하실 때 특별한 자질(지식, 의, 거룩함)을 주셨음을 나타내는 동사입니다. 인간의 능력은 자생적인 것이 아니라 창조주로부터 받은 것임을 강조합니다.
