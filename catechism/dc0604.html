Doctrine Exposition (교리 해설)

Today’s standard from the Westminster Confession of Faith (WCF) 15.4 reveals the depth of God's mercy towards penitent sinners. It beautifully establishes a double-sided truth of spiritual law and gospel assurance: "As there is no sin so small but it deserves damnation; so there is no sin so great that it can bring damnation upon those who truly repent." This profound formulation targets two dangerous misunderstandings: the dilution of God's holiness (legalism or moral categorization of sins) and the limitations placed upon Christ’s redemptive power (despair).

First, we must lay down the uncompromising standard of God’s holiness: there is no sin so small that it is exempt from eternal condemnation. In our human courts and moral paradigms, we tend to categorize offenses as "minor" or "major," assuming that trivial infractions do not warrant severe penalties. Historically, Roman Catholicism developed the distinction between mortal sins (대죄)—which destroy sanctifying grace and lead directly to hell unless confessed in the sacrament—and venial sins (소죄)—which are minor infractions that do not sever a believer’s relationship with God or require the same gravity of punishment.

Reformed theology fundamentally rejects this division. Sins are not measured simply by their visible outward consequences, but by the infinite majesty and holiness of the God against whom they are committed. Every sin, no matter how small or hidden (even a single "idle word" as warned in Matthew 12:36), represents an act of treason and rebellion against the Creator. James 2:10 asserts that keeping the whole law but stumbling in one point makes a person guilty of all. Therefore, in the absolute standard of divine justice, even the "smallest" sin is infinitely heinous and carries the penalty of spiritual and eternal death: "The wages of sin is death" (Romans 6:23).

The Infinite Offense vs. The Absolute Remedy

How can God be both perfectly just in condemning all small sins, and perfectly merciful in forgiving all great sins?

  • Infinite Offense: Because God's dignity is infinite, any sin against Him has infinite gravity. Human tears or temporal punishments can never balance the scales of divine justice.
  • Infinite Substitutionary Satisfaction: Christ, being both true God and true man, offered an obedience and a death of infinite value. Therefore, His sacrifice completely paid the debt for the most heinous offenses of those who repent.
  • The Comfort of Repentance: When a sinner is united to Christ, the Father no longer views them in their sin, but in the perfect righteousness of His Son. Thus, "there is now no condemnation" (Romans 8:1).

Second, the Confession delivers the ultimate gospel promise: there is no sin so great that it can bring damnation upon those who truly repent. This is a balm to the trembling conscience that fears it has sinned beyond the reach of divine mercy. Throughout scripture, we see God’s grace completely wash away crimes that human societies would deem unforgivable. King David committed adultery and murder; the Apostle Paul persecuted and executed the church; Peter denied Christ with curses—yet all were restored through true, heart-broken repentance and faith.

This is because the cleansing power of Christ’s blood is not measured by the magnitude of our guilt, but by the infinite merit of His cross. Isaiah 1:18 invites us with the promise that though our sins be as scarlet, they shall be made as white as snow. Sincere repentance is the fruit of a regenerating heart, and God will never turn away a broken and contrite spirit. Therefore, let no believer despair under the weight of their past transgressions, for the grace of the Gospel stands ready to welcome every returning child with a full and irrevocable pardon.

Grammar Focus 01

As there is no... so there is no...

"~인 ~가 없듯이, 마찬가지로 ~인 ~도 없다" (대칭 비교 구문)

이 구문은 고전 영문법 및 신학적 문서에서 두 상반되거나 상보적인 진리를 대조하여 균형을 맞출 때 자주 사용됩니다. 첫 문장의 부정적 한계가 진리이듯, 두 번째 문장의 은혜로운 현실 또한 확실한 진리임을 보여줍니다.
Vocabulary 02

Damnation / Condemnation

영벌, 정죄 (죄에 대해 내리는 형벌 선고)

Damnation은 지옥의 영원한 형벌(eternal punishment)을 의미하며, Condemnation은 법적이고 영적인 유죄 판결(정죄)을 가리킵니다. 죄는 본질상 하나님의 무한한 존엄을 모독하기에 이 선고를 받아 마땅합니다.
Theology Focus 03

Mortal Sins vs. Venial Sins

대죄(Mortal Sins)와 소죄(Venial Sins)

가톨릭 교리에서는 죄를 크기에 따라 대죄(영혼을 죽이고 은혜를 박탈함)와 소죄(가벼운 죄)로 나눕니다. 그러나 개혁주의는 모든 죄가 하나님의 무한한 거룩하심에 거스르는 무한한 악이므로 정죄를 피할 수 없다고 가르칩니다.
Vocabulary 04

Contrite / Penitent

상한 심령의, 깊이 통회하는

Contrite는 죄로 인해 완전히 깨어지고 부서진 마음의 상태를 뜻합니다. Penitent는 자신의 행동을 깊이 뉘우치며 하나님께 은혜를 구하는 회개자를 의미합니다.
Grammar Focus 05

But it deserves damnation

~하지 않을 정도로 (유사관계대명사 but)

부정어(no sin) 뒤에 쓰인 but은 'that ... not'의 의미를 가집니다. 직역하면 "지옥의 영벌을 받을 만하지 않은(but deserves) 그렇게 작은 죄는 존재하지 않는다(no sin so small)"는 뜻이 됩니다.
