Westminster Confession of Faith 9.3

Man, by his fall into a state of sin, has wholly lost all ability of will to any spiritual good accompanying salvation: so as, a natural man, being altogether averse from that good, and dead in sin, is not able, by his own strength, to convert himself, or to prepare himself thereunto.

Key Scriptures

"The mind governed by the flesh is hostile to God; it does not submit to God’s law, nor can it do so." (Romans 8:7)

"I am the vine; you are the branches... apart from me you can do nothing." (John 15:5)

"The person without the Spirit does not accept the things that come from the Spirit of God... and cannot understand them." (1 Corinthians 2:14)

01. The Total Inability of the Fallen Will

The Fall of Adam caused a fundamental change in human nature. Man did not lose the faculty of the will itself, but he lost the power to direct that will toward spiritual good.

  • A Spiritual Death: Being "dead in sin" means a complete lack of spiritual vitality. Just as a dead body cannot react to physical stimuli, a spiritually dead soul cannot respond to God's holiness.
  • B Focus on Salvation: This inability specifically concerns "spiritual good accompanying salvation"—actions that could merit God's favor or satisfy the debt of sin.

02. Natural Liberty vs. Moral Bondage

It is crucial to understand that even in sin, man's will remains naturally free. He is not forced to sin by an outside power; he sins because he wants to.

The Will is Voluntary

When a person chooses evil, they do so freely according to their desires. No one is coerced into rebellion against God; it is a spontaneous act of a corrupted nature.

The Analogy of Satan

Satan always chooses evil, not because he lacks a free will, but because his nature is so utterly perverse that he never desires the good. Fallen man shares this tragic inclination.

03. The Problem of the Natural Man

The "natural man" (unconverted) does not merely lack the power to do good; he has a total aversion to it.

  • Incapability of Self-Conversion: Because the will is enslaved to sin, man cannot decide to change his own fundamental nature or "prepare" himself for God's grace.
  • The Illusion of Civil Good: While non-believers can perform "civil good" (outwardly obeying laws), these acts are not "spiritually good" because they lack faith, a pure motive, and the goal of God's glory.
  • Need for Regeneration: Since the will is paralyzed toward the good, salvation must begin with God's sovereign act of giving a new heart (Ezekiel 36:26).
