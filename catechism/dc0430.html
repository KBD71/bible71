Westminster Larger Catechism 66

Q: What is that union which the elect have with Christ?

A: The union which the elect have with Christ is the work of God's grace, whereby they are spiritually and mystically, yet really and inseparably, joined to Christ as their head and husband; which is done in their effectual calling.

Key Scriptures

"...gave him to be head over all things to the church, which is his body..." (Ephesians 1:22-23)

"But he who is united to the Lord is one spirit with him." (1 Corinthians 6:17)

"I give them eternal life, and they shall never perish; no one will snatch them out of my hand." (John 10:28)

"For the husband is the head of the wife as Christ is the head of the church, his body, of which he is the Savior." (Ephesians 5:23)

01. A Sovereign Work of Grace

Union with Christ is not a human achievement; it is a gift from God. This union is accomplished through the instrument of faith, which is itself produced in the elect by the all-powerful work of the Holy Spirit.

  • Divine Initiative: God chooses the objects of His love not based on merit, but solely out of His own good pleasure and mercy.
  • The Spirit's Agency: The Holy Spirit creates the vital link between the believer and Christ during the effectual calling.

02. Spiritual, Mystical, and Inseparable

The nature of this union is defined by several profound characteristics that distinguish it from any earthly relationship.

Spiritual & Mystical

It is not a physical or material connection, but one mediated by the Spirit that transcends human reason and understanding.

Real & Inseparable

Though invisible, it is more real than any tangible bond. Once united, no power in heaven or earth can separate the believer from Christ.

03. Christ as Head and Husband

The Confession employs two powerful metaphors to describe the dynamics of this union:

  • The Head: As the Head of the body, Christ provides life, direction, nourishment, and protection to His members. The church depends entirely on His leadership and life-giving power.
  • The Husband: This represents the intimate, sacrificial, and covenantal love between Christ and His people. As a husband provides for and cherishes his wife, Christ gave Himself for the Church to make her holy and glorious.
  • Our Identity: Being the "body" and "bride" of Christ means our entire existence is rooted in His merit and sustained by His faithful affection.
