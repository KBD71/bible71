WCF 15.3. The Necessity of Repentance

"Although repentance be not to be rested in, as any satisfaction for sin, or any cause of the pardon thereof, which is the act of God's free grace in Christ; yet is it of such necessity to all sinners, that none may expect pardon without it."

Doctrine Exposition (교리 해설)

Repentance is a fundamental saving grace initiated within a sinner's heart by the Holy Spirit and the Word. In previous discussions, we established that a faithful gospel pulpit must seamlessly proclaim both faith and repentance. The Westminster Confession of Faith (WCF) 15.3 now guides us to a deeper, more refined understanding of the relationship between repentance, satisfaction, and the free grace of God. It warns us against two dangerous errors: legalism, which overvalues repentance as a meritorious cause of forgiveness, and antinomianism, which discards repentance entirely in the name of grace.

First, we must absolute maintain that repentance has no meritorious value whatsoever. Sins are forgiven solely because of the passive and active obedience of Jesus Christ—His suffering, death, and perfect righteousness—which are imputed to believers by faith. In the strict sense of divine justice, repentance cannot serve as a satisfaction (속상) for sin, nor does it function as the causal ground of pardon. When a criminal is caught and feels deep regret, his tears do not pay his legal debt to society; similarly, our tears of repentance do not satisfy the infinite wrath of God against sin. Seeking to rely on repentance as a payment for sin actually insults the all-sufficient sacrifice of Jesus.

The Roman Catholic Error vs. Reformed Orthodoxy:

Historically, the WCF directly opposes the medieval Roman Catholic sacrament of penance, which splits repentance into contritio (contrition), confessio (confession), and satisfactio (satisfaction).

  • Meritum de Congruo (재량공로): Roman Catholic scholasticism posits that human efforts, aided by grace, have a fittingness or congruous merit that makes it appropriate for God to reward them with forgiveness.
  • Meritum de Condigno (적정공로): This refers to condign merit, where an act holds intrinsic value equal to the reward under strict justice.
  • The Reformed Correction: Reformed theologians rejected both categories of merit. Our repentance is not a transaction; rather, it is a fruit of the Holy Spirit. Sins are pardoned purely as an act of God's free grace in Christ.

Second, despite its total lack of merit, the Westminster Confession declares that repentance is of such necessity to all sinners that "none may expect pardon without it." Repentance is not the *instrument* of justification (that role belongs solely to faith), but it is the *inseparable companion* and evidence of saving faith. True, living faith always turns toward Christ, which dynamically necessitates turning away from sin. To hope for divine forgiveness while persisting in unrepentant rebellion is an illusion and an abuse of the gospel.

Therefore, repentance is a lifelong, ongoing grace. Just as sanctification is a progressive reality whereby the seeds of repentance grow and strengthen, the believer's daily life is characterized by a recurring cycle of recognizing sin, mourning over its offensiveness to God, and running back to the cross. Repentance is the primary vehicle through which we live out our sanctification and walk in new obedience with God.

Theological & English Notes (신학 및 영어 학습 노트)

Vocabulary 01

Satisfaction

속상(贖償) / 죗값을 치름

공의로운 법적 요구를 완전히 '만족시키는 것'을 의미하는 신학 용어입니다. 죄에 대한 완전한 사죄적 배상은 오직 그리스도께서 십자가에서 흘리신 보혈과 순종으로만 충족(satisfy)되었습니다.
Vocabulary 02

Be not to be rested in

~에 안주하거나 신뢰해서는 안 된다

고전 영문법의 'be + to' 용법으로, 의무나 금지를 뜻합니다. 즉, 회개라는 주관적인 신자의 반응이나 슬픔 그 자체에 안심하여 그것을 공로로 착각해서는 안 된다는 뜻입니다.
Vocabulary 03

Free grace

값없이 베푸시는 은혜

죄인의 어떠한 조건이나 공로도 요구하지 않고 무조건적이며 주권적으로 베풀어 주시는 하나님의 전적인 은혜를 의미하는 개혁주의 핵심 표현입니다.
Theology Focus

Congruous Merit

재량공로 (Meritum de Congruo)

중세 가톨릭 신학에서 신자가 은혜의 보조를 받아 행하는 도덕적 행위가 엄격한 정의는 아닐지라도, 하나님의 자비하심에 따라 '사죄나 보상을 얻기에 적절하다'고 인정받는 공로 개념입니다. 개혁교회는 이를 전면 배격합니다.
Grammar Focus

Yet is it of such necessity...

양보절 이후 주절의 도치 구조

"Although ~"로 시작하는 양보절 뒤에서 강조를 위해 주어와 동사가 도치된 형태입니다. 'of + 추상명사(necessity)'는 형용사 'necessary'처럼 작용하여 "매우 필수적이다"를 격조 있게 나타냅니다.
