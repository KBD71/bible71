Westminster Confession of Faith 9.5

The will of man is made perfectly and immutably free to good alone, in the state of glory only.

Key Scriptures

"...until we all reach unity in the faith... and become mature, attaining to the whole measure of the fullness of Christ." (Ephesians 4:13)

"...to the spirits of the righteous made perfect." (Hebrews 12:23)

"...we know that when Christ appears, we shall be like him, for we shall see him as he is." (1 John 3:2)

01. The Culmination of Spiritual Restoration

The fourth and final state of the human will is the "State of Glory." While believers in the state of grace possess the ability to choose the good, they still struggle with remaining corruption. In glory, this internal conflict is finally resolved.

  • A Spirits Made Perfect: In our heavenly home, we will exist as "righteous ones made perfect," completely free from even the desire for sin.
  • B The Measure of Christ: We will attain the "fullness of Christ," being filled with the Holy Spirit and conformed perfectly to His image.

02. The Doctrine of Non Posse Peccare

In the state of glory, the will is fixed. It is "perfectly and immutably free to good alone." This is the state of being "unable to sin."

Just as the natural man in the state of sin is "unable to do spiritual good," the glorified saint is "unable to choose evil." There will be no desire, no temptation, and no possibility of falling back into sin. This is the ultimate security of the believer.

03. The Perfection of True Liberty

Some might ask: "If I can only choose the good, am I still free?" The answer is a resounding Yes.

  • Divine Liberty: God is the freest of all beings, yet He always does what is good because He is good. In glory, our freedom becomes like His.
  • The Freedom of Love: Our will is not coerced; rather, we will so love God and holiness that we would never want to choose anything else.
  • True Emancipation: This is the "glorious liberty of the children of God"—a freedom that finds its highest expression in voluntary, joyful, and eternal service to the King.
