Participation and the Spirit's Application

Shorter Catechism 30; Larger Catechism 59

W. Shorter Catechism 30

Q: How doth the Spirit apply to us the redemption purchased by Christ?

A: The Spirit applieth to us the redemption purchased by Christ, by working faith in us, and thereby uniting us to Christ in our effectual calling.

W. Larger Catechism 59

Q: Who are made partakers of redemption through Christ?

A: Redemption is certainly applied, and effectually communicated, to all those for whom Christ hath purchased it; who are in time by the Holy Ghost enabled to believe in Christ according to the gospel.

Key Scriptures

"...having believed, you were marked in him with a seal, the promised Holy Spirit, who is a deposit guaranteeing our inheritance..." (Ephesians 1:13-14)

"All those the Father gives me will come to me... and I shall lose none of them, but raise them up at the last day." (John 6:37, 39)

"...so that Christ may dwell in your hearts through faith." (Ephesians 3:17)

01. Producing Faith: A Divine Work

Participation in redemption is not a result of human will or merit. It is exclusively the work of the Holy Spirit. He initiates our journey by "working faith in us."

  • A Not a Human Product: Faith is not an act we generate from within our own psychology. It is a spiritual grace bestowed upon the soul by the Holy Spirit.
  • B Regeneration First: Faith is impossible without the grace of regeneration. As Jesus taught, one must be "born of water and the Spirit" to enter the Kingdom.

02. The Certainty of Effectual Calling

The Spirit does not merely invite us; He "effectually calls" us. This term emphasizes that the Spirit's work is sovereign and guaranteed to succeed.

  • Unstoppable Grace: When the Spirit calls the elect, the result is certain. It is not a mere possibility or a proposal that we might reject.
  • The Instrument of Word: The Spirit uses the Gospel message to call us, opening our eyes to see the beauty of Christ so that we come to Him willingly and joyfully.

03. The Foundation of Union with Christ

The ultimate goal of the Spirit's application is to unite the believer to Jesus Christ. Faith is the instrument that effects this spiritual bond.

Union with Christ is the ground for all subsequent benefits of redemption. Because we are "in Christ," there is now no condemnation (Romans 8:1). Like branches in a vine, our ability to bear spiritual fruit depends entirely on this union, which is made real in us by the Holy Spirit through faith.
