Westminster Confession of Faith 9.4

[English Version]

When God converts a sinner, and translates him into the state of grace, He frees him from his natural bondage under sin; and, by His grace alone, enables him freely to will and to do that which is spiritually good; yet so, as that by reason of his remaining corruption, he does not perfectly, nor only, will that which is good, but does also will that which is evil.

[Korean Version]

하나님께서 죄인을 회심케 하셔서 은혜의 상태로 옮기실 때, 죄 아래 있는 본성의 속박에서 죄인을 자유롭게 하시며, 오직 은혜에 의하여 영적으로 선한 것을 자유롭게 원하고 행할 수 있게 하신다. 그럼에도 그는 자신에게 남아 있는 부패성 때문에 선한 것을 온전히 원하지는 않는다. 곧 선한 것을 원하면서도, 또한 악한 것을 원한다.

01. Liberation from Natural Bondage

In the state of sin, the human will is enslaved to a corrupt nature. However, when God initiates the work of regeneration and conversion, He effectively breaks the chains of this bondage. By translating the sinner into a state of grace, God restores the capacity to love Him and seek His righteousness.

This transformation is a sovereign act of God. It is not something achieved through human merit or effort, but is given by grace alone. Through this grace, the believer is finally empowered to freely choose and perform what is spiritually good—a feat impossible for the natural man.

02. The Conflict of Remaining Corruption

A vital truth to understand is that regeneration does not immediately result in sinless perfection. Even though a person is moved into the state of grace, remaining corruption persists within them.

This leads to a spiritual paradox: the believer truly desires the good, yet simultaneously finds themselves desiring what is evil. The Apostle Paul describes this internal warfare in Romans 7, lamenting that "the evil I do not want to do—this I keep on doing." This struggle is a characteristic mark of the Christian life on this side of heaven.

03. Defining the Regenerate Will

The difference between the state of innocence and the state of grace is significant. While Adam was "able not to sin," the regenerate believer is enabled to choose the good despite being previously "unable not to sin."

  • Voluntary Response: The believer's obedience is a free response to God's love, not a coerced act.
  • Spirit-Led Desire: The desire for spiritual good is evidence of the Holy Spirit's indwelling presence.
  • Hope of Glory: This ongoing struggle motivates the believer to look forward to the state of glory, where the will will be immutably fixed on the good alone.

Theological & English Notes

1. Natural Bondage (본성의 속박)

타락한 인간의 의지가 죄에 매여 있어 스스로는 결코 하나님이 기뻐하시는 선을 선택할 수 없는 상태를 의미합니다. 'Bondage'는 노예 상태나 속박을 뜻하는 강력한 단어입니다.

2. Remaining Corruption (남아 있는 부패성)

중생한 신자라 할지라도 이 땅에 사는 동안에는 여전히 죄의 성향과 부패한 본성이 남아 있음을 뜻합니다. 이것이 신자의 내적 갈등(영적 전쟁)의 근본 원인이 됩니다.

3. "Yet so, as that..." (그럼에도 ~하여)

앞서 언급한 긍정적인 변화(자유를 얻음)에도 불구하고, 여전히 존재하는 제한 사항(부패성)을 소개할 때 사용하는 고전적인 영문 구문입니다.

4. Translate (옮기다 / 옮겨지다)

일반적으로 '번역하다'로 쓰이지만, 신학적으로는 '상태나 장소를 완전히 옮기다'라는 뜻으로 쓰입니다. 흑암의 권세에서 아들의 나라로 옮겨진 결정적인 위치의 변화를 의미합니다.
