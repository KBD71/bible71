Westminster Confession of Faith 8.8

To all those for whom Christ has purchased redemption, He does certainly and effectually apply and communicate the same; making intercession for them, and revealing unto them, in and by the Word, the mysteries of salvation; effectually persuading them by His Spirit to believe and obey, and governing their hearts by His Word and Spirit; overcoming all their enemies by His almighty power and wisdom, in such manner, and ways, as are most consonant to His wonderful and unsearchable dispensation.

Key Scriptures

"All those the Father gives me will come to me, and whoever comes to me I will never drive away." (John 6:37)

"In him we have redemption through his blood, the forgiveness of sins, in accordance with the riches of God’s grace." (Ephesians 1:7)

"Sanctify them by the truth; your word is truth." (John 17:17)

"And having disarmed the powers and authorities, he made a public spectacle of them, triumphing over them by the cross." (Colossians 2:15)

01. Specific and Effectual Application

Today's doctrine explains why not everyone is saved despite the infinite value of Christ's work. The effect of redemption is applied specifically to those for whom Christ purchased it.

  • A Particular Redemption: Christ did not just make salvation "possible" for all; He ensured its "certain and effectual" application for the elect given to Him by the Father.
  • B Divine Sovereignty: Unlike universal atonement, which places the final decision on human choice, limited atonement attributes the sovereignty of salvation entirely to God's election.

02. How Redemption is Communicated

The Confession outlines five specific ways Christ applies His redemptive work to His people:

1. Constant Intercession

As our High Priest, Christ appears before the Father to ensure His merits are applied to every believer.

2. Revelation through the Word

He unveils the "mysteries of salvation," teaching us the truth of the Gospel through His Word.

3. Effectual Persuasion

The Holy Spirit works within us to overcome our resistance, leading us to believe and obey.

4. Governing the Heart

Christ rules the inner lives of believers, conforming our wills to the law and will of God.

03. Total Victory over All Adversaries

Finally, Christ secures our redemption by conquering every enemy that opposes His kingdom and His people.

  • He subdues sin, Satan, the world, and death.
  • His victory was manifested at the cross, where He disarmed spiritual authorities.
  • This triumph is applied through His "almighty power and wisdom," ensuring that not a single one of His sheep is lost.
