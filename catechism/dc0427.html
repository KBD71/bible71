Westminster Larger Catechism 57

Q: What benefits hath Christ purchased by his mediation?

A: Christ, by his mediation, hath procured redemption, with all other benefits of the covenant of grace.

Key Scriptures

"For no matter how many promises God has made, they are 'Yes' in Christ. And so through him the 'Amen' is spoken by us to the glory of God." (2 Corinthians 1:20)

"He did not enter by means of the blood of goats and calves; but he entered the Most Holy Place once for all by his own blood, thus obtaining eternal redemption." (Hebrews 9:12)

01. The Biblical Meaning of Redemption

In common language, "restraint" or "confinement" is often associated with the word 'redemption' in certain contexts. However, the biblical meaning is the exact opposite.

  • Liberation through Ransom: Redemption means to set someone free from bondage by paying a specific price, known as a ransom.
  • Spiritual Freedom: Christ redeemed His people who were enslaved to sin and death by paying the ultimate price—His own precious blood.
  • Substitutionary Work: As the Mediator, Christ gave His life as a "ransom for many" (Matthew 20:28), delivering us from the curse of the law (Galatians 3:13).

02. The Satisfaction of Divine Justice

A common misconception is that the ransom was paid to Satan. This is incorrect. The ransom was paid to God the Father to satisfy His holy justice.

God is perfectly just and cannot simply overlook sin without a penalty. However, in His infinite love, He provided the very sacrifice His justice required. Christ's death is the propitiation that harmonizes God's love and justice, allowing Him to be both "just and the one who justifies" those who have faith in Jesus (Romans 3:26).

03. The Wealth of Covenant Benefits

Redemption is the foundation, but the mediation of Christ brings an entire treasury of spiritual blessings under the Covenant of Grace.

  • Justification: Being declared righteous.
  • Adoption: Becoming children of God.
  • Sanctification: Being made holy.
  • Glorification: Eternal life in glory.

These benefits include assurance of God's love, peace of conscience, joy in the Holy Spirit, and the strength to persevere in faith through life's trials.
