W. Shorter Catechism 82

Q: Is any man able perfectly to keep the commandments of God?

A: No mere man since the fall is able in this life perfectly to keep the commandments of God, but does daily break them in thought, word, and deed.

W. Larger Catechism 149

Q: Is any man able perfectly to keep the commandments of God?

A: No man is able, either of himself, or by any grace received in this life, perfectly to keep the commandments of God; but does daily break them in thought, word, and deed.

Key Scriptures

"Indeed, there is no one on earth who is righteous, no one who does what is right and never sins." (Ecclesiastes 7:20)

"For I know that good itself does not dwell in me, that is, in my sinful nature. For I have the desire to do what is good, but I cannot carry it out." (Romans 7:18)

"The Lord saw how great the wickedness of the human race had become... and that every inclination of the thoughts of the human heart was only evil all the time." (Genesis 6:5)

01. The Definition of Perfect Obedience

To "perfectly keep" the commandments means to obey every single law, at every moment, in heart, thought, and action, motivated entirely by love for God.

  • ! While Adam in his state of innocence had the ability to obey, no human since the Fall possesses this power.
  • ! Even the most revered saints—Mary, Joseph, Peter, or Paul—could not achieve perfection. Their sincerity is unquestioned, but sincerity is not perfection.

02. Sin in Thought, Word, and Deed

True obedience requires more than just avoiding outward "big sins." It demands a pure inner life.

Jesus taught that murder and adultery begin in the heart (Matthew 15:19). Because we still possess a fallen nature, evil thoughts often arise before they ever manifest in words or deeds. Therefore, even if a person could control their outward behavior, they would still fail the test of perfect holiness in their mind and spirit.

"If we claim to be without sin, we deceive ourselves and the truth is not in us." (1 John 1:8)

03. The Residual Sin in the Believer

A vital distinction must be made: the regenerate (born-again) person does not live in habitual, unrepentant sin (1 John 3:9), yet they are not sinless.

  • Indwelling Sin: As the Apostle Paul lamented in Romans 7, even those who love God's law find a "law of sin" at work in their members. The desire to do good is present, but the performance is hampered by remaining corruption.
  • Inherited Nature: The fundamental reason for this inability is the original sin inherited from Adam. We are born with a bent toward autonomy and away from God.
  • Hope in Christ: This doctrine is not meant to discourage, but to point us away from self-righteousness and toward the perfect righteousness of Christ, which is our only plea before God.
