Westminster Shorter Catechism 82

[English Version]

Q: Is any man able perfectly to keep the commandments of God?

A: No mere man since the fall is able in this life perfectly to keep the commandments of God, but does daily break them in thought, word, and deed.

(그 누구도 스스로의 힘으로, 혹은 이 생애에서 받은 어떤 은혜로도 하나님의 계명을 완전히 지킬 수 없습니다...)

01. The Standard of Perfect Obedience

To "perfectly keep" God's commandments implies an unceasing, absolute adherence to the Law in heart, mind, and action. This requires a motivation purely rooted in love for God. While Adam, in his state of innocence, possessed the capacity for such obedience, humanity lost this power after the Fall.

Even the most devout saints, such as Peter or Paul, did not achieve sinless perfection. Their sincerity was real, but sincerity is not perfection. God's Law remains a perfect mirror that exposes our continual need for a Savior.

02. Sin in Thought, Word, and Deed

The Catechism emphasizes that we break the commandments daily. This happens not only through external actions but also through our internal world.

  • A Inward Sin: Jesus taught that sin begins in the heart (Matthew 15:19). Anger can be murder, and lust can be adultery in the eyes of God.
  • B The Law of Sin: Paul described the "law of sin" at work in his members, where the desire to do good is present, but the power to carry it out perfectly is lacking.

03. The Reality of Residual Sin

A critical distinction exists between a non-believer and a regenerate believer. The born-again person does not live in habitual, unrepentant sin. However, because we still live in "this life," the corrupt nature remains.

"If we claim to be without sin, we deceive ourselves and the truth is not in us" (1 John 1:8). This doctrine prevents self-righteousness and drives us to depend entirely on the imputed righteousness of Christ. In Him, our imperfect obedience is covered, and we find our peace.

Theological & English Notes

1. Mere man (한낱 사람 / 평범한 인간)

신학적으로는 '죄 없는 참 인간'이신 예수 그리스도를 제외한, 아담 이후의 모든 타락한 인류를 지칭할 때 쓰이는 표현입니다.

2. In thought, word, and deed (생각과 말과 행동으로)

죄의 전포괄성을 나타내는 관용구입니다. 겉으로 드러나는 행동(deed)뿐만 아니라 입술의 고백(word), 그리고 마음의 동기(thought)까지도 하나님의 기준에 미치지 못함을 뜻합니다.

3. Imputed Righteousness (전가된 의)

우리가 스스로 만들어낸 의가 아니라, 그리스도의 완벽한 순종의 공로를 하나님이 우리의 것으로 여겨주시는(count/reckon) 것을 의미합니다. 우리의 무능력을 해결하는 유일한 길입니다.

4. Devout / DEVOTION (경건한 / 헌신)

본문 해설에서 쓰인 'devout'은 하나님을 경외하고 충성스러운 상태를 의미하지만, 아무리 경건한 사람이라도 이 땅에서는 불완전함을 강조합니다.
